# PRODUCT.md: thraxel-templates

## What it is

Thraxel's template gallery: one fixed sample page (Thraxel's own services page: AI agents, web builds, automation) rendered in 71 different design languages across 9 categories, each drawn from the visual system of a well-known tech brand. Customers browse the gallery, compare directions side by side, and pick a starting point for the project Thraxel builds for them. Will eventually attach to the main thraxel.io site.

## Register

Brand (marketing/gallery site). The shell itself is editorial and restrained by design so it frames the 71 template treatments without competing with them.

## Users

- Thraxel's tech customers and prospects (founders, CTOs, product teams) choosing a visual direction for a site or product Thraxel will build, who want to see one concrete page rendered across many designs rather than abstract swatches.

## Positioning

One sample page, every design, side by side, so choosing a direction has an easier starting point. The value is comparability: because the content never changes, every visual difference is the design talking. The homepage states this plainly in the first screen and stays short.

## Primary actions (conversion)

The gallery's job is to get a customer to a shortlist and then to thraxel.io with template names in hand:

- Browse the category sections and the grid of 71 template cards.
- Open a template's rendered `/preview/<slug>` page (all 71 are live) and the brand's real site for context.
- Follow the hero, navbar, About, or footer links to thraxel.io to start a project.

The frozen sample page is 13 sections (nav, hero, features, feature-detail rows, developer/API, brand specimen, stats, social proof, pricing, FAQ, CTA, contact form, footer), all rendered per template. The contact form is inert by design and sends nothing; the pricing tiers, specimen board, and FAQ are equally showcase surfaces, not commerce. They exist to show how each design styles inputs, feature lists, code, disclosures, and its own tokens.

## Brand personality

Quiet, editorial, curatorial. A print room or type-specimen catalogue, not a SaaS landing page. The site is the neutral wall; the 71 designs are the artwork.

## Design principles

- Frame, do not compete. The shell stays neutral so template treatments read clearly against it.
- Comparability is sacred. The sample page content is identical across all 71 renders; only styling changes.
- Editorial restraint over decoration. Measured spacing, real typographic hierarchy, one accent.
- Say what it is, fast. The homepage states its purpose in the first screen, plainly, and stays short. No first-person voice, no count in the headline.
- Follow the shared design laws: OKLCH color, no side-stripe accents, no gradient text, no glassmorphism-by-default, no identical-card grids by reflex, no em dashes.
